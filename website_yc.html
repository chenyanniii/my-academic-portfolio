Research Interests

My research program bridges molecular biology, quantitative ecology, and data science to understand plant resilience across biological scales.

Multi-omics & Molecular Mechanisms

Linking leaf hyperspectral reflectance to gene expression, I investigate how plant genomes and transcriptomes enable adaptation to environmental stress.

  • Established high-throughput protocols for wild-collected tissue (RNA/DNA extraction).
  • Generated large-scale RNA-Seq datasets linking physiological traits to expression.
  • Utilized machine learning (CNNs) to identify spectral signatures of genetic variation.

Phylogenomics & Macroevolution

Evolution of Angiosperms in response to climate change. I use comparative phylogenomics to understand how evolutionary history shapes current trait distributions.

  • Investigated gene family evolution across multiple plant species.
  • Linked genetic patterns to observed phenotypic traits.
  • Provided insights into evolutionary convergence and divergence.

Community Resilience

I link molecular evolution to community ecology by exploring how plant populations collectively respond to heat, water limitation, and other stressors.

  • Evaluated gene families across coexisting species in field communities.
  • Correlated functional trait variation with genomic signatures.
  • Applied findings to predict community-level responses to climate change.
